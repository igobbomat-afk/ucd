1. Research Delivery (Methods & Sessions)	Delivering user research activities to a good standard, using agreed approaches, and contributing reliably to a wider research programme.	Understands and applies common user research methods in familiar contexts.	• Applying qualitative and quantitative methods such as interviews, usability testing, or surveys in line with the goals set by the research lead.

• Being able to explain, at a functional level, why a particular method is being used and what kind of insight it is intended to produce (e.g., Attitudinal/Behavioural, Generative/Evaluative, Moderated/Unmoderated, Remote/In-person)

• Adjusting approach within sessions when needed (e.g. probing further on unclear responses, clarifying tasks for participants).
		Runs research sessions confidently using existing plans.	• Moderating interviews or usability tests independently using a provided discussion guide.

• Managing session flow, time, and transitions between activities without intervention.

• Responding calmly and appropriately to unexpected issues during sessions (e.g. participants going off‑topic, technical issues, unclear task comprehension).
		Delivers research in line with agreed processes, policies, and expectations.	• Applying consent, safeguarding, and recording processes consistently across sessions.

• Following agreed approaches to recruitment, data handling, and documentation without needing step‑by‑step instruction.

• Recognising when a situation falls outside agreed standards and flagging it to a more senior researcher.
		Contributes to the smooth running of research activity beyond the session itself.	• Preparing research materials in advance (e.g. guides, prototypes, observation notes) and ensuring they are ready for use.

• Supporting scheduling and logistics for multiple sessions, not just single interviews.

• Identifying practical delivery risks early (e.g. low recruitment numbers, tight timelines) and escalating appropriately.
		Works effectively alongside others to deliver research outcomes.	• Sharing clear and relevant observations with the research lead after sessions.

• Taking on responsibility for specific delivery tasks within a research programme.

• Incorporating feedback between sessions to improve delivery quality or consistency.
			
2. Analysis & Sense-Making	Making sense of user research data, identifying meaningful patterns, and contributing to clear, evidence‑based insights with guidance.	Captures research data in a way that supports later analysis.	• Producing clear, structured notes from interviews or usability tests that reflect what participants said and did.

• Recording observations accurately and consistently across multiple sessions.

• Distinguishing between participant behaviour, participant opinion, and researcher interpretation when writing notes.
		Actively takes part in analysing research data as part of a team or supporting an initiative.	•Taking part in collaborative analysis sessions (e.g. tagging notes, grouping observations, affinity mapping, survey analysis and coding).

•Identifying repeated issues, behaviours, or needs across sessions and calling them out during analysis.

•Sense-checking understanding with others when patterns are unclear rather than making assumptions.
		Begins to move from observations to insight, with support.	• Suggesting possible themes or problem statements based on patterns in the data.

• Articulating why a finding matters to users or the service, not just what was observed.

• Testing early interpretations with more senior researchers to validate or refine them.
		Uses research data to support conclusions and discussions.	• Linking observations back to specific sessions or user quotes when discussing findings.

• Helping ensure insights are grounded in evidence rather than assumptions or personal opinion.

•Noticing when evidence is thin or inconsistent and flagging this to the team.
		Contributes to shaping findings into usable outputs.	• Drafting sections of findings summaries or insight slides for review.

• Helping refine wording so findings are clear, accurate, and focused.

• Responding constructively to feedback on analysis and applying it in later work.
